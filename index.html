IPSN Toolkit

Pathogen genomic surveillance frameworks, tools, and guidance

The IPSN Toolkit is a suite of WHO-endorsed resources developed with global partners to help countries plan, implement, and sustain pathogen genomics for public health decision-making. It is coordinated by the International Pathogen Surveillance Network (IPSN), hosted by the WHO Hub for Pandemic and Epidemic Intelligence in Berlin.

Tools are organised across three domains — Capacity, Financing, and Data — and are targeted to programme managers, funders, laboratory professionals, policymakers, and other decision-makers.

About the IPSN Toolkit

The International Pathogen Surveillance Network (IPSN) is a global network of pathogen genomic actors hosted by the WHO Hub for Pandemic and Epidemic Intelligence. IPSN accelerates progress on the deployment of pathogen genomics to improve public health decision-making in line with the Global Genomics Strategy for Pathogens with Pandemic and Epidemic Potential.

The toolkit is created by IPSN partners, for the whole network. Resources span WHO-driven products, partner-driven tools, and IPSN Secretariat–supported outputs. All WHO-branded products undergo full WHO clearance processes before publication.

For further information, contact the IPSN Secretariat.
